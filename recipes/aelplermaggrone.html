Älplermaggrone

Description

Älplermagronen is a dish from the Alpine regions of Switzerland, consisting of pasta, potatoes, cream, cheese, and onions. The name is made up of "Älpler" as a designation for the Alpine herder and "Magronen", which was taken as a loan word from the Italian word

Ingredients

  • 2 Onions
  • 2 Garlic cloves
  • 400 g Firm-cooking Potatoes
  • 150 g Bacon cubes
  • 5 dl Vegetable broth
  • 2 dl Half-and-half cream
  • 250 g Pasta (e.g., Älplermagronen)
  • 60 g Grated Gruyère cheese
  • 0.5 tsp Salt
  • A pinch of Pepper

Cooking Steps

  1. Peel onions, garlic, and potatoes. Cut onions into rings, garlic into slices, and potatoes into cubes.
  2. In a non-stick frying pan, brown onions, garlic, and bacon until golden. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. In a separate pan, bring vegetable broth and half-and-half cream to a boil. Add potatoes and pasta, cover, and simmer over low heat for approximately 10 minutes until the pasta is al dente and all the liquid is absorbed.
  4. Stir grated Gruyère cheese into the potatoes and pasta, season to taste, and distribute the mixture onto plates.
  5. Arrange the set-aside bacon mixture on top.
